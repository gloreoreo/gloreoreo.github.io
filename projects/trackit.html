Trackit

January 2017 | Champaign, IL

Trackit is a conceptual application that is a universal package tracking utensil, designed with small businesses in mind. I had focused heavily on usability, which decisions were influenced by usability testing, along with data gathered during market research. This application was primarily designed for android in end of 2016.

Mindmapping

I began the design process by throwing my thoughts onto paper by mindmapping any problems, ideas, concerns and questions to address. I had also worked out potential UIUX directions, including list and icon organization, and ways to display progress of a package en route.

Market Research

To explore these questions, I had then conducted market research on existing products, like Fedex package tracking, and Microsoft Delivery Beta. I had analyzed the UI and design decisions they had made, and the effect it had on their consumers. Quotes featured are but few of the reviews I had encountered.

Research to Design

After conducting user and market research, I had began to make design decisions in terms of layout, progression, and notification methods.

Final Thoughts

Due to the experimental nature of the project, I had only done one low-fidelity prototype of two screens of the application, applying the information that I had gathered to the screens. Although small, I became rather proud of this project because it was the first time I had experimented with applying my Industrial Design expertise into UX design, and launched my career and growing interest into UX and Product Design. It also showcases a belief of mine, that research and user-testing are essential steps in a strong design process.
